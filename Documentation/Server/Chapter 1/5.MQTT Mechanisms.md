## 5. MQTT Mechanisms 

To ensure efficient and reliable communication between devices, MQTT uses several essential mechanisms. These contribute to connection management, error detection, and message delivery guarantees.

### Keep Alive 

**Definition**: The *Keep Alive* mechanism ensures that both the client and the broker are aware that the other party is still active and connected.

**Operation**: When a client connects to the broker, it can set a *Keep Alive* time interval, expressed in seconds. This represents the maximum allowable period of inactivity before a status check is required.



**PING Packets**: If no other packets have been sent during the specified interval, the client will send a *PINGREQ* packet to announce to the broker that it is active. The broker responds with a *PINGRESP* packet to confirm reception.

**Disconnection**: If the broker does not receive any packets from the client (including *PINGREQ*) within the established *Keep Alive* interval, it will consider the client disconnected and close the connection.

---

### Last Will and Testament (LWT) 

**Definition**: *Last Will and Testament* (LWT) is a special message that the client configures upon connection, which the broker will publish in the event of an unexpected disconnection.

**Configuration**: At connection, the client can define a "last will message," a "last will topic," a QoS level, and a retain flag.

**Operation**: If the client disconnects without sending the appropriate *DISCONNECT* packet, the broker will publish the *last will message* on the *last will topic* with the preset QoS and retain flag settings.

**Utility**: This mechanism allows other devices in the network to be notified when a client disconnects unintentionally, enabling them to take appropriate action (for example, to log an error or adjust the operation of other devices).

---

### Quality of Service (QoS) 

**Definition**: *Quality of Service* (QoS) is a mechanism that defines the level of reliability in message delivery between the client and the broker.



**QoS Levels**: 

* **QoS 0 – At most once**: The message is transmitted without any confirmation from the recipient. The message may not arrive at all or could be delivered multiple times in case of retransmission issues at other layers.
* **QoS 1 – At least once**: The message is delivered with confirmation, guaranteeing that the recipient receives it at least once. However, due to the confirmation mechanism, duplicate messages may occur.
* **QoS 2 – Exactly once**: This is the highest level of reliability. It uses a four-step handshake mechanism to ensure the message is delivered and processed exactly once, without duplication.
