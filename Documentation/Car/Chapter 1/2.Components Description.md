## 2. Components Description
### For the realization of this project, carefully selected hardware components were used, offering both the necessary functionalities and flexibility in implementation and testing. The main components of the system are presented below:

### 1. Raspberry Pi Pico W ("Pico 2W")
#### It is the main microcontroller of the car, based on the RP2040 chip. The board is a revised version of the Raspberry Pi Pico W, commercially labeled "Pico 2W", and integrates:
- two ARM Cortex-M0+ cores at 133 MHz;
- 264 KB of SRAM and 2 MB of flash memory;
- Wi-Fi connectivity through an upgraded wireless module (revision 2);
- a compact format, easy to integrate into a mobile embedded system.
#### This board controls the general logic of the system, reads data from the sensors, manages movement, and performs communication via the MQTT protocol with an external server.

### 2. 28BYJ-48 Stepper Motors (2 pieces)
#### They are used for the car's propulsion. These motors allow precise control of rotation and speed, making them suitable for smooth and controlled movement on the track. Main characteristics:
- 64 steps per revolution (512 with the internal reduction);
- increased torque through the gearbox;
- 5V power supply.

### 3. ULN2003 Drivers (2 pieces)
#### Each motor is controlled by a ULN2003 driver, which contains a set of Darlington transistors. These drivers receive signals from the Pico board and generate the necessary current for properly powering the motor coils.

### 4. 3D Printed Chassis and Wheels
#### The mechanical structure of the car is made entirely by 3D printing, tailored to the project's needs. It includes:
- a chassis for mounting components,
- two 3D printed wheels, connected to the motors,
- universal PCB with the role of stably supporting the sensors and the board.

### 5. HC-SR04 Ultrasonic Sensors (2 pieces)
#### Using ultrasonic technology, these sensors can measure the distance to objects. They are used for:
- detecting obstacles in front of the car;
- detecting traffic lights, as predefined stopping points.

#### The operating principle is based on emitting an ultrasonic pulse and measuring the time until the reflected echo is received.

### 6. Line Sensor (manually built)
#### For tracking the route, a simple but efficient line sensor was built, composed of:
- an infrared LED that illuminates the ground;
- two photodiodes, which detect the reflected light.

#### The difference in reflection between the black line and the light background allows the identification of the correct position on the track and the adjustment of the driving direction.
